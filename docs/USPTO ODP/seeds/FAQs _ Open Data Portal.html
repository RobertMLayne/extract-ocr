About the Open Data Portal (ODP)

What is the Open Data Portal (ODP)?

The Open Data Portal (ODP) is our new hub for open data, built on the idea of finding several datasets in one platform so anyone can easily search and extract the data they need. The ODP is a unified platform that offers public patent data in one place. Enhanced APIs and robust documentation help improve the entire data experience.

Customer demand for our data has grown rapidly since the launch of the original Open Data Portal initiative in 2015 from 50 users to millions. The new Open Data Portal is the next step in the USPTO journey to amplify the public value, accessibility, and efficiency of patent data sharing. Improved Patent Examination Data System (PEDS) capabilities, Bulk Data Storage System (BDSS), and Developer Hub features and datasets will be included in this tool.

We are continuously making improvements to ensure the reliability, efficiency, and quality of the data and overall service for all customers.

With new customer feedback, iterative design, and continued development, well add new datasets and features to ODP to meet your needs.

What data is available in ODP?

ODP provides access to patent data made available to the public as set forth in37 CFR 1.11 and 1.14. This includes datasets from the Patent Trial and Appeal Board (PTAB), Patent File Wrapper (PFW), Final Petitions Decisions (FPD), and Bulk Data Storage System (BDSS).

  • Patent Trial and Appeal Board (PTAB) includes trial and appeal proceeding public data.
  • The Patent File Wrapper (PFW) are datasets previously found in the Patent Examination Data System (PEDS), including publicly available patent applications, bibliographic/front page, and patent-related data fields.
  • Final Petitions Decisions (FPD) includes the same datasets as the previous Final Decisions Commissioner for Patents, such as final agency petition decisions in patents and published patent applications.
  • ODPs Bulk Data Directory replaced the previous Bulk Data Storage System (BDSS), which includes statis bulk datasets.

Over time, additional datasets and functionality will become available as new services are folded into ODP, including Trademark Decisions and Proceedings, Office Actions, and more. New features to support these new datasets will also be continuously added. Refer to the Release Notespage for details on what services and functionality have been transitioned to ODP.

Why are you creating a new Open Data Portal?

Customer demand for USPTO data has grown rapidly since the launch of the Open Data Portal initiative in 2015. In recent years, customer feedback has demonstrated the need for a central portal of USPTO data.

Customer feedback helped the team design and develop the sites features.

As part of our commitment to fostering a culture of open government, ODP supports the broader goals of solving customer data needs while improving the customer experience as a High-Impact Service Provider.

Ultimately, we can better serve the public and the intellectual property community by offering tools to efficiently discover, access, and use our public data for new innovation. This effort supports American innovation and competition that drives our economy forward.

Open Data Portal (ODP) and Developer Hub (Open Data Portal beta)

How does the new Open Data Portal (ODP) differ from the Developer Hub

The Open Data Portal is an evolution of our Developer Hub.

The original Open Data Portal Beta or Developer Hub was a 2015 initiative to improve the discoverability, accessibility, and usability of public patent and trademark data. It was a successful initiative increasing customer demand for USPTO data. The momentum of Developer Hub led to the creation of several public-facing data services.

Through lessons learned from the Developer Hub and customer research efforts, the USPTO identified the need to make navigating and accessing USPTO data easier and more efficient. Previously, customers had to access multiple platforms or create workarounds to find data they needed that wasn't available in specific datasets. The new ODP builds on the same goals of the originalimproving access to USPTO datanow with additional customer research and feedback to improve your experience.

Eventually, we hope you will be able to access all USPTO datasets from the ODP platform to find everything you need. Today, you can already access the Patent Trial and Appeal Board (PTAB), Patent File Wrapper (PFW), Final Petitions Decisions (FPD), Patent Examination Data System (PEDS) and Bulk Data Storage System (BDSS) data through ODPs user-friendly design, adjustable datasets, and enhanced APIs and documentation. You can more easily refine your searches and extract the exact data you need with the flexibility and customization you want, and you can leverage APIs and documentation for a more encompassing and accessible data extraction experience.

Will the Developer Hub be retired because of ODP?

Developer Hub will eventually retire. However, ODP and Developer Hub will run in parallel through 2025 while we collect additional customer feedback and make improvements to ensure ODP meets your individual needs.

Open Data Portal (ODP) and Patent Trial and Appeal Board (PTAB)

What PTAB data from the DH PTAB is available in ODP?

All of the trial and appeal proceeding data from the DH PTAB will be migrated over to ODP PTAB. This contains Patent AIA trials (Trial Proceedings, Trial Decisions, Trial Documents), Appeals and Interferences data.

What functionality is different in ODP PTAB vs. in the DH PTAB?

The ODP PTAB data is not changing and there will not be new attributes, but how the data is organized and how you can search the data are being updated.

The APIs in ODP PTAB will be broken down by either Trials, Appeals, or Interferences. Trials are also broken down further by either Trial Proceedings, Trial Decisions, or Trial Documents.

The updated search feature enables users to search within a data set, across the above fields: Trial Proceedings, Trial Decisions, Trial Documents, Appeals, and Interferences, while linking the search to the Patent Center. Users will also still be able to use the existing Global Search feature.

Will the DH PTAB be retired because of ODP?

The DH PTAB will be retired because of ODP. Users will be directed to ODPs PTABs feature page to search, view, and extract trial and appeal information. The new ODP PTAB will run in parallel with DH PTAB to ensure that you have plenty of time and resources to acclimate to the new tool before DH PTAB data is eventually retired.

Open Data Portal (ODP) and Final Petition Decisions - Commissioner for Patents

How does the Open Data Portal (ODP) differ from the Final Decisions - Commissioner for Patents webpage?

ODPs Final Petition Decisions feature will contain final agency petition decisions in patents and published patent applications that were filed in 2001 or later. This feature will have the same datasets as the Final Decisions - Commissioner for Patents, but with improved attributes and better functionality. Users will be able to search, view, and extract any available Final Petition Decisions from the Commissioner for Patents in one place. Users will also be able to utilize ODPs Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) to extract datasets.

Will the Final Decisions - Commissioner for Patents webpage on the USPTO FOIA page be retired because of ODP?

The Final Decisions Commissioner for Patents webpage will still be accessible to the public but will no longer be updated to include additional petition decisions. Users will be directed to ODPs Final Petition Decisions feature page to search, view, and extract final decisions on matters related to patent applications and patents from the Commissioner for Patents. Petition decisions that were previously stored on the Final Decisions Commissioner for Patents webpage, including those in patents and published patent applications that were filed prior to 2001, will remain archived on the webpage.

What are Final Petition Decisions?

A petition is a written request for the USPTO to take a certain action in your patent or patent application. The denial decisions contained in ODPs Final Petition Decisions feature are final agency actions, or the conclusion of the USPTOs consideration of the petition or request. Depending on the petition type and status of the application or patent, judicial review of the decision may be available. See the Manual of Patent Examining Procedure (MPEP) 1002.02 for additional information.

Open Data Portal (ODP) and Patent Examination Data System (PEDS)

What data from the Patent Examination Data System (PEDS) is available in ODP?

PEDS data is established as the Patent File Wrapper in ODP. From the Patent File Wrapper web interface, you can search and extract the entire dataset containing the data for all indexed documents (up to 3,000 records at a time) or you can select just the data you are interested in by adjusting the dataset for desired attributes. Alternatively, you can use the APIs to extract the data you need. The data can be accessed by anyone using the Patent File Wrapper web interface or the provided APIs with your personal API Key.

The Patent File Wrapper Search in ODP contains publicly available patent applications, bibliographic/front page, and patent-related data fields filed from January 2001 to the present, with ODP refreshed daily. They are sourced from the USPTO file storage center, Patent Application data hub, and Patent Assignment systems. You can also navigate to Bulk Datasets > Patent File Wrapper (Bulk Datasets) to access bibliographic and static patent data compiled into file sets. You can access the Entire Datasets (10-year increments) and Delta Datasets (daily increments) to download in bulk within ODP.

What functionality is different in ODP Patent Data vs. in the Patent Examination Data System (PEDS)?

We interviewedPEDScustomers to understand their needs for the new Open Data Portal. As a result, you can:

  • Easily refine your searches to retrieve the data you want, not just fixed bulk formats:

    • Search across several attributes from one search bar to extract the data you need
    • Customize the data attributes youd like to view
    • Download only the bulk data you want (not an entire bulk dataset that you don't want), including records from search results in both CSV and JSON
    • Conduct Boolean queries to search across patent attributes

  • Easily navigate the interface, making data easy to access regardless of skillset:

    • View search results via a new, modern user interface (UI)
    • Quicker navigation for browsing through patent applications and details, including easily moving from patent to patent using previous and next buttons when viewing patent application details
    • View the front-page image of patents and download PDFs of PGPub and Granted Patents
    • Quick access to API syntax of search results

  • Experience enhanced APIs and documentation for more encompassing and accessible data extraction:

    • Leverage improved API documentation with clear examples of JSON responses and attribute descriptions to expertly request and receive patent data via API
    • Learn how to use APIs to perform the same functions from PEDS for improved efficiency, including a new process for accessing the data you downloaded from PEDS in bulk
    • Use an API Key to manage site traffic for an improved experience for all users reducing or eliminating site outages caused by excessive downloads
    • See what data was updated in ODP since the last time you extracted data

  • See improved data quality, efficiency, and overall service operability with a more reliable and resilient service:

    • Data is preprocessed, cleansed, and analyzed
    • Dates have been standardized to the international format (Day/Month/Year) for consistency with International Organization for Standardization guidelines
    • Schema enforcement makes data consistent
    • Transactions ensure that only completed writes are committed
    • Time travel maintains versions of data
    • And more

The ODP launch with PEDS data sets the stage for future implementation, integrating data sources and services as we add additional USPTO datasets to the same platform over time.

Will the Patent Examination Data System (PEDS) be retired because of ODP?

As of March 14, 2025, Patent Examination Data System (PEDS) is no longer available. To access publicly available records of USPTO patent applications or patent filing status, users can access ODPs Patent File Wrapper feature.

Use our PEDS transition guide to get you accustomed to the Patent File Wrapper interface and utilizing the Application Programming Interfaces (APIs).

Please feel free to provide suggestions for improvement in the User Feedback Survey, and Contact us with any additional questions.

ODP only has patent data from 2001 to the present. Where can I access patent application data prior to 2001?

To retrieve data prior to January 2001 that you previously found on PEDS, you can visit the Patent Center (for data similar to what is found in ODP) or find the static files in Bulk Data Directorys Patent Examination Data System (Bulk Datasets) - XML and Patent Examination Data System (Bulk Datasets) - JSON.

I used to be able to download the entire USPTO dataset in XML. Why cant I do this in ODP?

To provide all users with these improvements, we needed to change how data is downloaded. Whereas on PEDS, a user could download the entire USPTO dataset in JSON or XML files from the homepage (in flat, static files) and create custom patent dataset downloads, users can now access this data in JSON format via API instead.

Patent File Wrapper

How can I use the Patent File Wrapper API to download documents/files?

Look at Example 4 on the API syntax example page to learn how to download documents/files using the API.

Note that downloading large files may be delayed. If you are unable to download any large files, send us a message on our Contact us page.

Additionally, ODP uses an HTTP redirect 301 to provide a direct link to download files and you may need to add an extra parameter (allow/follow redirects) based on your software library. For example, in Python to allow redirects, you can add allow_redirects=True to your request;. In Curl, you can use -L or -location option to enable redirect following.

Can I download the entire (static) dataset or a dataset that I have customized?

You can download the entire USPTO dataset (in flat, static files) by navigating to the Patent File Wrapper (Bulk Datasets) page. You can view and download entire patent datasets along with delta datasets under the respective tabs on this page as well. Note that data will no longer be available in XML files; it will be available in JSON format.

In the meantime, you can still access the entire dataset programmatically (via the API).

As for downloading a customized dataset, custom downloads put a severe burden on USPTO systems and caused system outages that meant PEDS wasn't accessible to all users. You can still access this data, but for the USPTO to provide this free service and ensure it is available and accessible to all users and integrate it with the ODP design, we needed to change how you access it. Whereas on PEDS, a user could create custom patent dataset downloads, users will now be able to access a downloadable file of patent data in two different ways:

(1) Programmatically (in JSON format) via API

You will want to use this method to download large datasets (i.e., more than 3000 records). Follow these steps for extracting customized, bulk data using ODP APIs:

  1. If you haven't already, follow the steps in the Getting started section to create a USPTO.gov account and obtain your API Key
  2. Use the ODP API Search endpoint (https://api.uspto.gov/api/v1/patent/applications/)
  3. Use Swagger UI . to help you format your request and use the table below to know which data attributes you want to include in the JSON response

(2) Customize select data attributes to download Patent File Wrapper data

You can use this method if you want to look for certain filters and download based on certain data attributes. You can use this method if you want to download 3,000 records or less. Follow these steps for downloading data via the download modal:

  1. Navigate to Patent File Wrapper UI to search for applications or grants.
  2. Use the Search Filter panel to view available filter options
  3. Select the files you want to download
  4. Select the download button to select or deselect data attributes to include in your download
  5. Download a customized Patent dataset

For example, if you would like all records related to the application type label "Utility," you input "searchText": "applicationTypeLabelName:Utility," and then add any sorts, filters, pagination, etc., that would help you review the data response.

For example, 

 
"filters": [
    {
      "name": "applicationStatusDescriptionText",
      "value": [
        "Patented Case"
      ]
    }
  ],
  "rangeFilters": [
    {
      "field": "issueDate",
      "valueFrom": "2010-08-04",
      "valueTo": "2022-08-04"
    }
  ],
  "sort": [
    {
      "field": "filingDate",
      "order": "desc"
    }
  ],
  "fieldList": {
    "action": "include",
    "fields": [
      "applicationNumberText",
      "firstNamedInventor",
      "patentNumber"
]
  },
  "pagination": {
    "offset": 0,
    "limit": 25
  },
  "facets": [
    "applicationTypeLabelName",
    "applicationStatusCode"
  ],
  "downloadType": "json"
}

If you have any questions about formatting your request to receive the customized dataset you are used to, please review our FAQs, API Syntax Examples, and Swagger UI , and Contact us as needed. Bulk Data Directory

I want to programmatically download the zip files from Bulk Data Directory, but I get the Please use redirect URL to download error. What do I need to do?

To download a bulk data file, you need to use an option to allow redirect. -L

In case of CURL, you need to follow this example:

curl -L -H "x-api-key: your-api-key" 
https://api.uspto.gov/api/v1/datasets/products/files/TRTDXFAP/apc250421.zip -o apc250421.zip

In case of Python, use this example below:

url = 'https://api.uspto.gov/api/v1/datasets/products/files/TRTDXFAP/apc250421.zip'
response = requests.get(url, allow_redirects=True)

How can I form a downloadable URL for a bulk data product file to download?

There are two options to identify or form a downloadable file URL.

Option 1:

Go to web page of the Product that you are interested, see the file pattern and form the downloadable url:

Ex: Go to Trademark Full Text XML Data (No Images) Daily Applications and see the files are structured in the format apcyymmdd.zip

To download a file from this product (TRTDXFAP), the file downloadable URL would be

https://api.uspto.gov/api/v1/datasets/products/files/TRTDXFAP/apc250421.zip

Once you know the URL, you can download it using the API Key like the below CURL example. Please note that you need to use -L option to follow the redirect.

curl -L -H "x-api-key: your-api-key"  
https://api.uspto.gov/api/v1/datasets/products/files/TRTDXFAP/apc250421.zip -o apc250421.zip

Option 2:

Make a product metadata api call that returns the file downloadable urls.

Ex: For the same product trtdxfap (Trademark Full Text XML Data (No Images) Daily Applications), make a product metadata call like this -

curl X GET https://api.uspto.gov/api/v1/datasets/products/trtdxfap -H "x-api-key: your-api-key"

Which will return the JSON response that contains the fileDownloadURI.

Can I submit more than one API request at a time? What happens if I exceed my weekly download quota?

No, you cannot make more than one API request at a time. If you submit more than one request at a time or exceed weekly quota, you will get the error message: HTTP 429 Too Many Requests. Therefore, it is best practice to handle an HTTP 429 error gracefully. We strongly discourage automatic retries as this may exacerbate the issue. When you get a HTTP 429 response, try again using at least a 5 second delay between requests.

It is best practice to remedy a HTTP 429 error with a try catch block. Please refer the API Syntax Examples page for code samples.

If you exceed your download quota, you will receive an HTTP 429 error. To continue making API calls, you will need to wait seven days from the time you first started using the API key to try again.

API Key

What will require an API key?

Open Data Portal provides free and non-authenticated access to data through our bulk files and user interfaces. ODPs APIs provide developers with programmatic access and require an API key.

Why is an API Key required?

To manage the load on the system and ensure access to USPTO data is available and fair to everyone, an API Key will be required to access USPTO data using APIs.

How do I get an API Key?

To get an API Key, you must sign in to the MyODP page with your USPTO.gov account, validate, and link your ID.me account. Follow the steps on the Getting started page to create your API Key.

Why do I need a USPTO.gov account to get my API key?

MyUSPTO is where you can actively manage your intellectual property portfolio. You can track patent applications and grants, check trademark registrations and statuses, and access our services in your personalized USPTO gateway.

Why do I need an ID.me account to get my API key?

Verification enhances our security for online services by ensuring the identities of individuals performing actions related to our filing and registering processes. This helps to protect sensitive information while preventing fraud.

Linking your ID.me account to your USPTO.gov account is a one-time registration. Once completed, you will be able to access your API Key anytime.

What if I have multiple ID.me accounts? Which one should I link?

A duplicate account occurs if you verified your identity on a different ID.me account. Since a verified account is tied to you as an individual, you can only have one account.

If you already created a duplicate ID.me account using your work email address or another email address you own, follow the steps inHow to use an email address on your duplicate accountto make your email address available to add to your original ID.me account.

For more information on duplicate accounts, visitWhat to do if you have a duplicate account.

Can I have multiple API keys with one ID.me account?

No. The ID.me account integration with your USPTO.gov account is a one-to-one connection. You can only integrate your ID.me account into one USPTO.gov account. If you need to replace your API Key, email data@uspto.gov a request for a new API Key https://help.id.me/hc/en-us/articles/4412245504407-USPTO-and-ID-me on your personal MyODP page.

I'm an international user and ID.me requires documents I do not have. What do I do?

You must verify your identity on a video call if you live outside the United States. To learn how to do this, visit the Verifying if you live outside of the United States page.

What if I lose access to my ID.me account?

Review ID.me's Help Center and contact their team for support.

What if my employee leaves and I need to reassign an ID.me account?

An ID.me account cannot be reassigned as an API key is assigned by person, not organization. For a new employee, a new API key must be created. Follow the Getting Started guidance on how to create a new API key. Note that the employee leaving the organization can deactivate and reactivate their API key on their personal MyODP page.

What if I need to block an ID.me account from accessing the API key?

You can deactivate your API key in your personal MyODP page.

Our company would like to share one API key for the whole company. Can we make an exception and get a company API Key instead?

No. An API key is by person, not organization, and verified through a personal ID.me account. We cannot make exceptions for organizations as the personal identifier verification process enhances our security for online services by ensuring the identities of individuals performing actions on behalf of organizations. This helps to protect sensitive information, while preventing fraud.

Im having trouble verifying my ID.me. Who can I contact to support this?

If you have any questions regarding ID.me, review ID.mes Help Centerpage. If you have any further questions, please visit our Contact uspage.

What law or regulation requires the USPTO to use ID.me to verify the identity or authenticate users prior to using USPTO API services?

While there is no single law or regulation that specifically requires the use of ID.me, the USPTO has adopted ID.me for identity verification and authentication purposes to comply with a framework of laws, regulations, and guidelines designed to secure federal information systems and protect sensitive agency data and user privacy. The Federal Information Security Modernization Act of 2014 (FISMA), 44 U.S.C. 3551 et seq., requires federal agencies to implement information security protections commensurate with the risk and magnitude of the harm resulting from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction of information collected, used, or maintained by a federal agency or contractor. FISMA also requires federal agencies, among other requirements, to investigate incidents, audit information systems, and implement certain policies, standards, and guidelines for safeguarding federal information systems. One such policy is the Office of Management and Budget (OMB) Circular A-130, which further requires federal agencies to deploy effective security controls, such as user identification and authentication tools, to balance the potential security risk of public access systems and services that is consistent with the protection of privacy. Additionally, the USPTOs use of ID.me follows NIST Special Publication 800-63 (four-volume suite), Digital Identity Guidelines, which provides technical requirements for digital identity services implemented by federal agencies.

The USPTOs use of ID.me supports compliance with all of these requirements and guidelines for offering its use of digital API services that is commensurate with the agencys information system risks. For more information regarding the agencys risk assessment, see Privacy Impact Assessment. We only collect identifying information to the extent that is legally authorized and necessary for the proper performance of agency functions in providing these services to the public. The USPTO also continues to provide free and non-authenticated access to patent data through our bulk files and user interfaces.
